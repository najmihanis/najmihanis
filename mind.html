TARGET

Designed a palm oil transplanting attachment concept for a farm buggy system focusing on retrieval, handling, and placement workflow constraints in the field.

CONSTRAINTS
  • Irregular polybag geometry at the seedling interface
  • Bag-dependent opening force and insertion angles
  • Variation in seedling size and mass affecting handling stability
  • Harsh operating conditions with dirt and moisture exposure

WHAT

Designed a conceptual transplanting attachment for automated transplanting of palm oil seedlings.

System features:

  • Hydraulic-powered plant retriever to transfer seedlings to a central conveyor.
  • Integrated conveyor system for precise plant movement.
  • Polybag remover with self-closing, semi-flexible fingers to strip polybags after cutting.
  • Earth auger drilling mechanism for automated hole creation.
  • Fertilizer dispensing system integrated with planting mechanism for efficient seedling placement.
HOW
  • Analyzed and worked with existing SolidWorks assemblies and parts.
  • Researched mechanisms and materials, including linear actuators, airless-tire-inspired structures, spring-loaded probe arrays, and compliant mechanisms.
  • Designed the mechanism by consolidating and refining multiple early concept directions.
  • Performed analysis to select motor, chain, sprocket, and hydraulic specifications based on design constraints.
  • Prototyped polybag remover fingers using various material options.
  • Created motion simulations and rendered demonstration videos in SolidWorks.
