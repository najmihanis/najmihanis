What?
  • Designed and fabricated a 3-axis robotic wrist for a Mars rover for the University Rover Challenge (URC).
  • Engineered the wrist to deliver high output torque with low backlash, enabling stable and precise end-effector positioning.
  • Developed the mechanism under strict packaging, load, and reliability constraints, with the expectation of real-world field operation.
  • Iterated the design through multiple revisions to improve shaft support, bearing placement, and structural robustness.
  • Collaborated with cross-disciplinary teams to integrate mechanical and electrical components
How?
  • Iterated the design through multiple revisions to improve shaft support, bearing placement, and structural robustness.
  • Evaluated multiple transmission architectures before converging on a timing-belt–driven reduction for precision and reliability.
  • Compared GT3 vs HTD belt profiles, selecting components based on backlash, load capacity, and manufacturability tradeoffs.
  • Calculated pulley sizes, belt lengths, and center-to-center distances to achieve proper tension without idler pulleys.
  • Designed and fabricated parts using SolidWorks (including sheet metal workflows) and laser cutting.
  • Selected bearings, shafts, pulleys, and fasteners with attention to alignment, serviceability, and sourcing availability.
  • Balanced precision, torque, backlash, manufacturability, and available off-the-shelf components in a constrained robotic subsystem.
