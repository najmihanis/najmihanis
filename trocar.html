Overview
What
  • Led R&D for an elastomer seal used in pneumatic trocar leakage test systems.
  • Developed a repeatable sealing solution balancing conformity, durability, and test consistency.
  • Benchmarked in-house seal performance against FDA-approved commercial references.
How
  • Evaluated elastomer behavior by relating durometer to elastic modulus and deformation under known pressure conditions.
  • Narrowed viable materials by comparing gas permeability, compression set, abrasion resistance, and surface conformity.
  • Iterated across material selection, mold design, fabrication, and testing to converge on a stable seal design.

Mold Design

  • Designed precision seal molds with tolerance stack-up analysis to control final seal height.
  • Simplified mold geometry to reduce defects on critical sealing features.
  • Applied surface finish considerations and clear dimension control to improve repeatability.

Seal Fabrication

  • Fabricated seals using silicone, polyurethane, and rubber with material-specific processing workflows.
  • Controlled degassing and curing conditions to minimize bubbles and surface defects.
  • Adapted procedures to material sensitivities including viscosity changes and working-time limits.

Seal Testing

  • Benchmarked seal performance using a differential pressure leak test rig.
  • Established reference baselines using glued trocars and FDA-approved seals.
  • Evaluated consistency and repeatability across materials and fabrication variants.

Challenges

  • Leak readings were highly sensitive to setup variables, increasing measurement variability.
  • Polyurethane processing had tight working-time limits and higher sensitivity to process conditions.
  • Bubble formation during degassing was amplified by small mold geometry and venting constraints.
