MISSION OVERVIEW
TARGET

Designed an automated demolding fixture for laparoscopic access platforms to reduce operator task time while maintaining compatibility with existing production machines.

CONSTRAINTS
  • Non-uniform mold body geometry
  • Strict alignment requirements with hard mechanical stops
  • Limited gripping surface for secure extraction
  • Tight tolerances and intricate geometric features
  • Required compatibility with existing claw machine interface
  • High extraction force required to remove luer pins

Ideation

Initial concepts explored how to work within the constrained geometry using purely mechanical locking mechanisms such as push-push. Evaluating the operator workflow revealed that the manual sequence per part would be impractical at scale, prompting a transition to pneumatic actuation.

Iteration

01 02 03 04
ITERATION 01
Manual Twist-Lock Mechanism
Using the tab as a cam feature to drive the locking prong outward.
  • Problem: Purely mechanical locking was hard to reset and relied heavily on operator precision, making it unsuitable for high-volume use.
  • Change: Introduced a spring-loaded side lock and rotational latch to guarantee full prong extension before release.
  • Result: Mechanism proved functionally viable but was too manual and ergonomically inefficient for production workflows.
ITERATION 02
PNEUMATIC-ASSISTED LOCKING
Replacing manual rotation with pneumatic actuation to reduce operator effort.
  • Problem: Manual actuation and rotational motion created fatigue and orientation issues for operators.
  • Change: Replaced spring-ejected prongs with pneumatic actuation while retaining mechanical tab anchoring.
  • Result: Improved actuation force consistency, but rotational ergonomics and missing pin-extraction capability surfaced as critical gaps.
ITERATION 03
FULLY PNEUMATIC RELEASE SYSTEM
Automating prong release and pin extraction for repeatable operation.
  • Problem: Previous concepts lacked an automated way to extract vertical luer pins and required too much operator alignment.
  • Change: Integrated dual pneumatic actuation for both tab anchoring and prong deployment, plus a vertical pin-removal cylinder.
  • Result: Achieved full automation and compatibility, but tight clearances and bulky 3D-printed geometry revealed usability and cost concerns.
ITERATION 04
LOW-PROFILE, OPERATOR-GUIDED DESIGN
Reducing bulk while improving clearance and ease of use.
  • Problem: Concept 3 worked mechanically but was unforgiving to load, manufacture at scale, and lacks alignment assistance.
  • Change: Simplified structure, increased clearances, removed unnecessary embosses, and added guiding features to support intuitive drop-in loading.
  • Result: Delivered a low-profile, clearance-safe demolder that reduced operator effort, improved reliability, and supported repeatable high-volume use.
